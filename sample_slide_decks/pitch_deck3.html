Introduction to Juice Wrld

Jarad Anthony Higgins, known as Juice Wrld, was an influential American rapper and singer-songwriter who made a significant impact in the emo rap and SoundCloud rap genres.

Early Life

Juice Wrld was born on December 2, 1998, in Chicago, Illinois. He began his music career in 2015 under the name JuicetheKidd.

Breakthrough with 'Lucid Dreams'

His breakout single "Lucid Dreams" peaked at number two on the US Billboard Hot 100 and was included in his debut album, "Goodbye & Good Riddance."

Albums and Collaborations

Juice Wrld released several successful projects, including "Wrld on Drugs" with Future and "Death Race for Love," which debuted at number one on the Billboard 200.

Tragic Death

Juice Wrld passed away on December 8, 2019, due to a drug overdose, leaving a legacy of emotional music that resonated with many fans.

Posthumous Releases

His first posthumous album, "Legends Never Die," achieved commercial success, featuring hits like "Come & Go" with Marshmello.

Legacy

Juice Wrld's music continues to influence artists and fans alike, solidifying his place in modern music history.
